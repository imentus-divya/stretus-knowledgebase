# Parabolic SAR flip

**Parabolic SAR** (“stop and reverse”) places dots above/below price to suggest **trend direction** and a **trailing stop** concept. A **flip**—dots moving from below to above price (or vice versa)—is often treated as a **regime change** signal.

## Overview

SAR accelerates as a trend progresses (parameters control sensitivity). It shines in **clean trends** and struggles in **sideways** markets.

## Signal definition

- **Bullish flip**: SAR dots move **below** price (implementation-specific).
- **Bearish flip**: SAR dots move **above** price.

Many traders require confirmation (e.g. close confirmation, volume).

## Parameters (typical)

| Parameter | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Step / acceleration | controls how fast SAR tightens |
| Maximum | caps acceleration |

## How to interpret

Treat SAR as a **risk management** tool as much as an entry trigger—stops can be wide early in moves.

## Risks and limitations

Choppy markets produce frequent flips. Not investment advice.
