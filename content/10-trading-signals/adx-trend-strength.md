# ADX trend strength filter

The **Average Directional Index (ADX)** measures **trend strength**, not direction. Traders often use ADX as a **filter**: only take trend-following signals when ADX confirms a **strong** trend—helpful on NSE names that alternate between trend and chop.

## Overview

ADX is derived from directional movement (+DI / −DI). High ADX suggests **persistent** directional movement; low ADX suggests **weak** or **range-bound** behaviour.

## Signal definition (as a filter)

- **Trend regime**: ADX **above** a threshold (commonly **20–25**, sometimes higher intraday).
- **No-trade / mean-reversion regime**: ADX **below** threshold (market may be choppy).

Direction is often taken from **+DI vs −DI**, not from ADX alone.

## Parameters (typical)

| Parameter | Common values |
|-----------|----------------|
| ADX period | 14 |
| Strength threshold | 20–25 |

## How to interpret

ADX **lags**; it confirms strength after a move has begun. Use it to **reduce** trades in unfavourable regimes rather than as a standalone entry trigger.

## Risks and limitations

ADX does not tell you *which* way price will go. Not investment advice.
