# CCI extreme reversion

The **Commodity Channel Index (CCI)** measures deviation from a moving average, scaled by mean deviation. Traders often highlight **extremes** beyond ±100 or ±200 as potential **mean-reversion** zones—useful only with regime awareness on NSE stocks and index futures.

## Overview

CCI is unbounded but **spends most time** in a typical band; extremes can indicate **stretch** (not guaranteed reversal).

## Signal definition

- **Long bias (mean reversion)**: CCI spikes **below** a very negative threshold then **turns up** (rules vary).
- **Short bias (mean reversion)**: CCI spikes **above** a very positive threshold then **turns down**.

Trend traders sometimes invert the logic—trade **with** extreme CCI in momentum regimes (higher risk).

## Parameters (typical)

| Parameter | Common values |
|-----------|----------------|
| CCI period | 20 |
| Extreme thresholds | ±200 (example) |

## How to interpret

CCI is noisy intraday; consider **higher timeframe** filters and **liquidity**.

## Risks and limitations

Extremes can extend in trends. Not investment advice.
