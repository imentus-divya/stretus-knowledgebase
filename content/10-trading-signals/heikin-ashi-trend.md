# Heikin-Ashi trend candles

**Heikin-Ashi** constructs candlesticks using averaged open/close/high/low, producing smoother charts. Traders often look for **sequences of candles without lower shadows** (uptrend) or **without upper shadows** (downtrend) as a **trend persistence** visual signal.

## Overview

Heikin-Ashi is **not** the same as standard OHLC candles—signals are **smoothed** and can **lag**.

## Signal definition (visual / rules-based)

- **Bullish trend cue**: consecutive **green** candles with **little/no lower wicks** (rules vary by platform).
- **Bearish trend cue**: consecutive **red** candles with **little/no upper wicks**.

Reversal candles (doji-like bodies) may signal **pause** or **exhaustion**—subjective without strict rules.

## Parameters (typical)

Uses the same timeframe as your chart; no extra period—**interpretation** is where traders differ.

## How to interpret

Confirm with **volume**, **levels**, and **higher timeframe** bias. Great for communication, dangerous if used alone.

## Risks and limitations

Smoothing hides intra-candle risk. Educational only—not investment advice.
