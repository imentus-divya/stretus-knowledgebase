# Volume profile: point of control (POC)

**Volume profile** distributes traded volume across price levels. The **point of control (POC)** is the price level with the **most volume** for the session or lookback window—often treated as a **magnet** or **fair value** reference in intraday and swing analysis on liquid Indian instruments.

## Overview

POC is not a “prediction”; it summarizes **where** business was done. Traders watch **migrations** of POC over time and reactions at **value area highs/lows**.

## Signal definition (conceptual)

- **Return-to-value**: price moves away from POC then **reverts** toward it in range regimes.
- **Acceptance / rejection**: sustained trading **above** prior value can imply **revaluation**; failure back inside can imply **false breakout**.

## Parameters (typical)

| Idea | Notes |
|------|------|
| Session vs composite | session for intraday; composite for swings |
| Value area % | commonly ~70% around POC |

## How to interpret

Profile quality depends on **clean tape** and **liquidity**. Thin stocks can show distorted profiles.

## Risks and limitations

Auction concepts require practice. Not investment advice.
