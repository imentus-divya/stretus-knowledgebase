# Bollinger Band squeeze

**Bollinger Bands** wrap price with a moving average and volatility-based bands. A **squeeze** happens when bands narrow—often interpreted as **volatility compression** that may precede a larger directional move (breakout or expansion) on NSE-listed names.

## Overview

Bands widen when volatility rises and tighten when volatility falls. A squeeze is **not** a direction forecast by itself; it flags **potential energy**.

## Signal definition

- **Squeeze on**: band width (or standard deviation envelope) falls below a **threshold** vs recent history.
- **Trigger**: many traders wait for a **price close outside** the band *after* the squeeze, or combine with **volume** confirmation.

## Parameters (typical)

| Parameter | Common values |
|-----------|----------------|
| MA length | 20 |
| Std dev multiplier | 2 |
| Squeeze lookback | compare band width to ~100–250 bars |

## How to interpret

False breakouts happen—especially near major events (results, RBI, global risk). Use **liquidity** and **session** filters relevant to Indian market hours.

## Risks and limitations

Squeezes can resolve into chop. Educational only—not investment advice.
