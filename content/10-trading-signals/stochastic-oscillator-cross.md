# Stochastic oscillator cross

The **stochastic oscillator** compares closing price to the recent range. Traders often use **%K crossing %D** as a timing signal, sometimes combined with overbought/oversold zones for swing ideas on liquid Indian stocks.

## Overview

Stochastic highlights **where** price closed within its recent high–low range—useful for spotting short-term momentum shifts, especially in **range** regimes.

## Signal definition

- **Bullish**: fast line **%K** crosses **above** slow line **%D** from below an oversold region (optional filter).
- **Bearish**: **%K** crosses **below** **%D** from an overbought region (optional filter).

## Parameters (typical)

| Setting | Common values |
|---------|----------------|
| %K period | 14 |
| %D smoothing | 3 |
| Overbought / oversold | 80 / 20 (or 70 / 30) |

## How to interpret

In strong trends, stochastic can **embed** in overbought/oversold—crosses alone may fail. Pair with **trend filters** or higher timeframe structure.

## Risks and limitations

Intraday noise on thin names can distort readings. Educational content only—not investment advice.
