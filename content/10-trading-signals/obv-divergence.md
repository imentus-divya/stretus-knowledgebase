# OBV divergence

**On-Balance Volume (OBV)** cumulates volume by adding/subtracting based on whether price closes up/down. Traders watch **divergence**: price makes a new high/low while OBV does not—sometimes interpreted as **weakening** participation (early warning, not certainty).

## Overview

OBV is a **cumulative** series; level matters less than **direction and structure** vs price.

## Signal definition

- **Bearish divergence**: price **higher high**, OBV **lower high** (potential exhaustion—requires confirmation).
- **Bullish divergence**: price **lower low**, OBV **higher low** (potential absorption—requires confirmation).

## Parameters (typical)

OBV uses the **close rule**; some platforms offer variations. Confirm with **trendlines** or **peaks/troughs** over multiple swings.

## How to interpret

Divergences can **persist**—timing is hard. Combine with **levels**, **volatility**, and **risk management**.

## Risks and limitations

Corporate actions and splits affect price/volume series—adjust data carefully. Educational only—not investment advice.
