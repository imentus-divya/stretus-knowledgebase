# Ichimoku Cloud: Tenkan / Kijun cross

**Ichimoku** packs multiple lines and a **cloud (Kumo)** into one chart system. A common **signal** is the **Tenkan–Kijun cross** (“TK cross”), often interpreted with **price vs cloud** for directional context on daily charts of Indian equities.

## Overview

- **Tenkan-sen**: short-term equilibrium over ~9 periods (default settings).
- **Kijun-sen**: medium-term equilibrium over ~26 periods.
- **Kumo**: forward-shifted support/resistance zone from Senkou spans.

## Signal definition

- **Bullish TK cross**: Tenkan crosses **above** Kijun.
- **Bearish TK cross**: Tenkan crosses **below** Kijun.

Many practitioners require confirmation: price **above/below** the cloud, or **Chikou** confirmation.

## Parameters (typical)

Default Ichimoku settings are widely used (9, 26, 52, displacement 26)—adjust only with care and testing.

## How to interpret

Ichimoku is **information-dense**; avoid over-trading every cross. Consider **higher timeframe** trend and **liquidity**.

## Risks and limitations

Complex indicators still produce false signals. Educational only—not investment advice.
