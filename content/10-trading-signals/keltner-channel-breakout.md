# Keltner channel breakout

**Keltner channels** wrap price around an EMA with a band width often based on **ATR**. Traders look for **breakouts** above/below the channel—or **mean reversion** back toward the EMA—depending on style and market regime.

## Overview

A channel gives **context**: volatility expansion vs compression. Breakout traders often want a **close outside** the channel with **volume** confirmation on liquid NSE names.

## Signal definition

- **Breakout (trend)**: price **closes outside** the upper/lower channel and holds (rules vary).
- **Mean reversion**: fade extremes back toward the **middle line** when ADX is low / range regime.

## Parameters (typical)

| Parameter | Common values |
|-----------|----------------|
| EMA midline | 20 |
| ATR period | 10–20 |
| ATR multiplier | 1.5–2.5 |

## How to interpret

Channels are not magic lines—**false breakouts** happen around news and on illiquid stocks. Prefer **session rules** and **risk caps**.
