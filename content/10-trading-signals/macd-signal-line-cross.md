# MACD signal line cross

**MACD** combines moving averages into a momentum oscillator. A widely used **signal** is when the MACD line crosses the **signal line**—often treated like a compact trend/momentum trigger on charts of Indian equities and indices.

## Overview

MACD is typically built from **12/26** EMAs with a **9**-period signal line. Traders watch **crossovers** and **histogram** expansion/contraction.

## Signal definition

- **Bullish cross**: MACD line crosses **above** the signal line.
- **Bearish cross**: MACD line crosses **below** the signal line.

Some traders require the cross to occur **above/below zero** for stronger context.

## Parameters (typical)

| Component | Default (classic) |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Fast EMA | 12 |
| Slow EMA | 26 |
| Signal EMA | 9 |

## How to interpret

MACD is **lagging**—good for **persistent** trends, noisy in tight ranges. Consider aligning with higher timeframe bias (e.g. daily regime for intraday entries).

## Risks and limitations

Whipsaws around consolidation are common. Not investment advice—verify with your own process and risk controls.
