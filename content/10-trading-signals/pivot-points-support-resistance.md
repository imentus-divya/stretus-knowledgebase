# Pivot points: intraday support / resistance

**Pivot points** compute key levels from the **prior session’s** high, low, and close. Traders use **PP, R1/R2, S1/S2** as intraday reference zones—common in Indian market communities for planning **entries, targets, and stops** on liquid names.

## Overview

Pivots are **mechanical levels**—their edge comes from **self-fulfilling behaviour** when many participants watch the same numbers.

## Signal definition

- **Mean reversion**: fade moves into **extreme pivots** in range days (risky without confirmation).
- **Trend day**: price **opens and holds** beyond a pivot level and continues—some traders chase with strict risk.

Common variants: classic, Fibonacci, Camarilla, Woodie (verify formulas in your platform).

## Parameters (typical)

| Input | Notes |
|------|------|
| Prior day H/L/C | must match session definition (spot vs future) |

## How to interpret

Align pivot session with what you trade (cash vs current month future). **Gaps** can invalidate naive pivot logic.

## Risks and limitations

Pivots are not predictions. Not investment advice.
